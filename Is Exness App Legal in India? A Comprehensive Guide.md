## 2. Forex Trading Regulations in India

To determine whether the Exness App is legal in India, we must first examine the regulatory environment for forex trading. Forex trading in India is governed by the **Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), 1999**, enforced by the **Reserve Bank of India (RBI)** and the **Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI)**.

### Key FEMA Guidelines for Forex Trading:
- **Currency Pairs**: Indian residents are allowed to trade forex only in currency pairs involving the Indian Rupee (INR). These include USD/INR, EUR/INR, GBP/INR, and JPY/INR. Trading in cross-currency pairs (e.g., EUR/USD) is prohibited for Indian residents.
- **Authorized Platforms**: Forex trading must be conducted through brokers or platforms authorized by the RBI or SEBI. Unauthorized platforms may violate FEMA regulations.
- **Remittance Limits**: Indian residents can remit up to **$250,000 per financial year** for permissible transactions, including forex trading, under the Liberalized Remittance Scheme (LRS).
- **Taxation**: Profits from forex trading are subject to taxation under Indian income tax laws, typically as business income or capital gains.

### Role of SEBI:
SEBI regulates the securities and derivatives markets in India. While SEBI oversees brokers offering forex derivatives (such as currency futures and options), it does not directly regulate retail forex trading. Instead, SEBI-approved brokers must comply with RBI guidelines for forex-related activities.

Given these regulations, the legality of the Exness App hinges on whether it complies with FEMA and RBI guidelines.
